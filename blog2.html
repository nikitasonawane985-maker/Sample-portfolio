Accessibility in Entertainment, Opening Doors for All

To begin with, let us understand what accessibility means in the context of entertainment. Accessibility in entertainment ensures that all content consumed for entertainment purposes can be perceived and understood by everyone, including people with various disabilities.

This content can include movies, TV shows, music concerts, sports matches, and even social media content. Some ways to make this content accessible include providing audio descriptions and image descriptions for people with visual disabilities, subtitles or closed captions for people with hearing disabilities, and accessible entrances in movie theatres for people with mobility impairments.

As a person with a visual disability, I rely on image and audio descriptions to consume content, so I am going to discuss these features and how they have changed my content-consuming experience.

Imagine this: a movie is playing on television, and you can hear everyone reacting—laughing and crying—to the scenes. But all you can hear is the background music and a few dialogues without any context.

Or you come across a social media post with many likes, and people are expressing different reactions in the comments, but you cannot understand the post because the image lacks a description. This is what people with visual disabilities, including myself, experience when content is not made accessible.

Thanks to some streaming platforms and screen readers, people like me can access content more easily and independently than before.

Audio Description

Audio description involves narrating information about the visual elements in a video that are relevant to understanding the story. This can include character actions, appearance, behaviors, and surrounding settings.

Even though friends and family try to describe visual content, some content is highly visually driven and difficult to explain. A good example is the Bollywood movie Barfi, which revolves around a deaf and mute boy and an autistic girl.

Since the movie depends heavily on expressions, actions, and visual storytelling, someone with visual impairment may not fully understand it through casual explanation alone. Audio description makes a significant difference in such cases.

Watching content with audio description gives me a sense of independence. I can enjoy my favorite show without needing someone else to explain it.

Audio description allows me to imagine characters and scenes in my own way rather than through someone else’s perspective. This makes the viewing experience more meaningful and fulfilling.

Image Description

Recent screen reader updates, such as TalkBack image descriptions, have transformed how visual content is accessed. These tools can describe images, read text embedded in them, and even explain details such as outfits, surroundings, or visual settings.

Since social media depends heavily on visuals, many people with visual disabilities find it difficult to use independently.

Image descriptions help users understand memes, photos, and reels without constantly depending on others for explanations.

Points to Improve Accessibility Features

These points are based on my personal opinion and experience and may differ from others’ perspectives.

Audio description should primarily describe visual elements. However, on some streaming platforms, narrators translate dialogues spoken in another language into the video’s primary language.

For example, in a Hindi film, English dialogues may be translated into Hindi. This can sometimes suppress the original dialogue and affect meaning, especially when specific words or signature phrases are important to understanding the character.

Similarly, image descriptions should focus on presenting content accurately and allow people to interpret it for themselves instead of explaining emotions or intent.

The whole purpose of accessibility features is to convey the same information available visually. Inaccurate or unnecessary details can negatively affect the experience.

Conclusion

Accessibility features have helped people with disabilities experience the same world as everyone else.

They allow us to engage in trends, conversations, and entertainment more independently, making life more inclusive, meaningful, and connected.
